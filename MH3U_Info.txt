MH3U Trainer: There are just two tabs Replicate & Weapon
=====================================================================================
Replicate Tab:
Capture button: 
	In Village/Port: read player pouch and food skill.
	Outside Village/Quest: read player pouch, food skill, and player stats (health, stamina, etc.)
Write button:
 	In Village/Port: write modified data back to player pouch and food skill.
	Outside Village/Quest: write modified data back to player pouch, food skill, player stats (health, stamina, etc.), and active all the checkbox features.
Run button (replicator): 
	In Village/Port: Feature doesn't work in village/port.
	Outside Village/Quest: Button start by doing a capture, then write modified data back every x seconds, depending on what the time interval is set at.  You could modify the trainer data anytime while the replicator is running.  It'll just update the player data on the next time interval.  Replicator will give infinite of everything. 
Tips/Hints:
	Replicating Items: In game, go stand next to your chest. Put items you want to duplicate in your pouch. Hit capture button, then modified item amount, hit write button.  In game, now store item back to chest.
	Getting New Items: In game, go stand next to your chest. Empty your pouch, hit capture button.  Now look at the “Item IDs” list, determine what items you want, put the Item ID and amount you want in the trainer, hit write button. In game, now store item to chest.
	
	Money,Resource: Just give yourself x amount of Azure Drgnshpire (worth 32,000 each) or Dire Dragongem (worth 31,000 each), then sell it. Give yourself Lava Nuggest (1500 each), then trade it for resources points.
	Food skills: You could give yourself food skills in or outside village at any time.  Look at the list of “Food skill ID” to determine what you want.
	Time Interval: You shouldn't set the update time interval to be less than 3 seconds. 5 seconds is the default and should work fine. The larger the time interval, the more stable the game & replicator becomes.
	Health: The max health is 9999, but you don't need it that high.  With the replicator, if you set it to 5 seconds and your health at 300, there is no way ANY monster could take 300 health from you within 5 seconds. Especially if your defense is over 3000, which I'll explain later in the Weapons tab section.
	Weapon/Armor/Decoration: In game, empty your pouch, go to the blacksmith. Talk to him to find out what items you need to make that piece of weapon/armor/talisman.  Then hit capture button, now look at the “Item ID” list and determine what you need.  Set the amount you need, then hit the write button.  You should have all the item you need in your pouch.  Talk to the blacksmith again and buy that weapon/armor/decoration.  **Note: If you don’t see a piece of weapon/armor in the blacksmith’s list. It means you don’t have the first item that’s required for that piece of weapon/armor.  If that’s the case, then use Ping’s Dex to determine what you need and populate your pouch accordingly. 
	*** Warning *** Outside village/Quest: NEVER give yourself more than the default amount of items in your pouch. E.g. game will only allow 5 mega dash, hence NEVER give yourself more than 5.  The game does real time calculation of how much item you have left.  If the amount is greater then what it expects, the games just set the item amount to zero.  If you use the replicator, you shouldn't need to set the amount higher than the default because the replicator will replenish the items every x seconds.  
	***### Critical Warning ###*** Shut off replicator when the quest ends or returning to the village.  The game has a 50% chance of crashing if the replicator is on when the quest ends, because the replicator is accessing a memory region the game is trying to erase.
=====================================================================================
Weapon Tabs: 
	Read button: In game, find out the page, row (horizontal), and column (vertical) number the item you want to read.  Then in the trainer set the page #, Row #, and Col #, hit the read button.  Each weapon/armor/talisman have 4 32-bit data associate with it, which the trainer label as data 1, data 2, data 3, and data 4.  Look at “Weapon Instructions” for detail to each data set.
	Write button: The last read, will populate the write fields.  This is where you would modified each of the data.  Don’t forget to change the page, row, or col if you don’t want to overwrite the exist weapon/armor/talisman.

Tips/Hints:
	Weapon Instructions: Very important you read “Weapon Instructions” section carefully and make sure you understand it before doing any modification.  Read more than ones if you have too.
Decorations/Jewels: Each weapon, armor, and talisman have 3 spaces for jewel decorations, not slots.  The concept of slots is an in-game restriction.  You could populate any decoration in an armor piece, even if that armor piece doesn’t have any slots.  
Also, the slot requirement of a decoration doesn’t matter.  Since each piece of armor/weapon/talisman have 3 spaces, you could populate up to 3 Attack Jewel 3 (attack +5), giving you up to 15 points in attack on a single piece of armor.  
In total, there are 21 spaces (1 weapon, 5 armors, and 1 talisman).  Use the spaces wisely, such as only populate decoration with +3 or greater skill points.  Look in Ping’s Dex, under decorations to determine which skill set have the highest jewel points. E.g. Attack and Expert is cheap because both skill have a +5 jewel, while Evade Dist is very expensive because it only has a +1 jewel.  Hence if you want Evade Dist, then get it from an armor piece, such as Nargacuge Greaves X, which gives you +3 Evade Dist.
Talisman give you 2 instant skill, hence use this only for skills that have no jewel decorations, such as Status Res, Edgemaster, Steadyhands, etc.. Or you could use if for expensive skills such as Awaken or Evade Dist.
With the right mix of armors and decorations, you could have over 10 skill sets plus 3 food skills.

	Defense: Defense doesn’t really matter because you have the replicator and 9999 max health, but if you still want over 3000 in defense, then upgrade your armor level.  You could upgrade any armor piece to level 256 (which is level 0) and your defense per armor will be 500 or over.  The advantage of this leveling is that it’s permanent if you save it.  Your armor defense stays even if you don’t use the trainer the next time you play.
***### Critical Warning ###*** Game will crash if you modified armor/weapon/charm while it's equip. Make sure you unequip it before doing anything.  Hence, you should only do modification next to your chest and unequip everything, to be save.  ***Note: save your naked self as one of the equipment set. Then just equip that set when modifying armor/weapon/charm next time.  That way, you don’t forget anything.  It’s the safest way.
=====================================================================================
Strategy: 
Here is the recommended mix armor/weapon/talisman:
	Armors: Duramboros Cap X, Rath Soul Vest, Brachy Guards X, Barroth Legging Z, Rath Soul Coat Z
	Decorations: 2x (Hearing +4), 3x (Expert +5), 4x (Attack +5), 3x (Potential +3), 3x (Spirit +4), 2x (Survivor +3), 2x (Focus +4)
	Weapon: any kind
	Talisman: Blademaster (Status Res: Awaken or Elemental), Gunner (Status Res: Steady Hands)
The setup should give you:
	Blademaster: Attack (L), Critical Eye +3 (Expert), Adrenaline +2 (Potential), Fortify (Survivor), Challenger +2 (Spirit), Focus (Fast charge), HG Earplugs, Status Res, Awaken or Elemental
	Gunner: Attack (L), Critical Eye +3 (Expert), Adrenaline +2 (Potential), Fortify (Survivor), Challenger +2 (Spirit), Focus (Fast charge), HG Earplugs, Status Res, Steady Hands
Food skill: eat specialist for status, pyro and bombardier for slime.

In Game play: take advantage of Survivor and Potential.  Get yourself carted twice, so survivor kicks in, then set your health to be 3000/9999.  This will kick in Potential. Turn on the replicator.  With all the checkbox features, your attack now should be 5x of your base weapon attack. E.g. if your weapon has an attack of 300, now it’ll be close to 1500.  Imagine a dual blade 1500 damage.  Gunner will get 6x base attack because of the bullets/coatings.  Remember, the player status will not show 1500 because purple sharpness, critical eye, charge meter, etc. are hidden attack buffs, but they are there during in-game calculation.

Talisman:
Data1 == (aabbcccc)
aa = Talisman Type (From "Equipment ID" list, since this is a talisman aa = 06)
bb = Number of slots on the talisman (max = 03). Don't have higher then 03.
cccc = Talisman Level 0001-000A (levels 1 - 10). 0001 = pawn talisman, 000A = creator talisman

Data2 == (aabbccdd)
aa = Talisman Skill Ability #1 (From "Talisman Skill ID" list, such as 6D = potential)
bb = Skill Points for Talisman Skill Ability #1. Such as hex 0A = 10 skill points for potential.
cc = Talisman Skill Ability #2 (From "Talisman Skill ID" list, such as 12 = Expert)
dd = Skill Points for Talisman Skill Ability #2. Such as hex 14 = 20 skill points for Expert.

Data3 == (aabbccdd)
aa = 00, not sure what it does
bb = From "Jewel ID" list, such as 3E = hearing +4
cc = 00, not sure what it does
dd = From "Jewel ID" list, such as 3E = hearing +4

Data4 == (aabbcccc)
aa = 00, not sure what it does
bb = From "Jewel ID" list, such as 38 = attack +5 
cccc = 0000, not sure what it does
